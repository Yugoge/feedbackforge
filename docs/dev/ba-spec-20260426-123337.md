## Edge Cases & Risks

- **Risk**: Switching from `inline-flex` to `flex` on the inner divs could in theory cascade unintended block sizing if the children had assumed an `inline` context. They don't — the children are an `<svg>` icon, `<h1>`, plain `<div>` (the NC circle), and a `<span>`. All are happy in a block-level flex container. No risk.
- **Risk**: `<h1>` is already block by default; wrapping it in a non-flex inline container previously gave it the `inline-flex` parent's centering behavior. Once the parent is `flex`, `<h1>` still works fine — `flex` parents normalize their children to flex items regardless of the children's intrinsic display value.
- **Edge**: At very narrow viewports (e.g. 320px), the NC + long company text already wrap mid-line in baseline; this is unchanged by the fix and is acceptable (both rows still visibly stack).
